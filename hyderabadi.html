Hyderabadi Biryani

Ingredients

Chicken Marinade:

  1. Chicken: 1 kg (curry cut)
  2. Yogurt (Curd): 1.5 cups, whisked
  3. Ginger-Garlic Paste: 2 tbsp
  4. Red Chili Powder: 1 tbsp (Kashmiri preferred)
  5. Turmeric Powder: ½ tsp
  6. Biryani Masala: 1 tbsp
  7. Green Chillies: 2-3 (paste or slit)
  8. Fried Onions (Birista): 1 cup (2-3 medium onions, sliced and fried golden)
  9. Mint Leaves: ½ cup, chopped
  10. Coriander Leaves: ½ cup, chopped
  11. Ghee/Oil: 4 tbsp
  12. Salt: To taste
  13. Lemon Juice: From 1 lemon

Rice Preparation:

  1. Basmati Rice: 500g-750g (long grain, soaked 30-40 mins)
  2. Whole Spices: 1 Bay leaf, 4 Cardamom, 4 Cloves, 1-inch Cinnamon, 1 tsp Shahi Jeera
  3. Salt: 2-3 tbsp (water should taste salty)

Layering & Garnish:

  1. Saffron Strands: Soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk
  2. Ghee: 2-3 tbsp
  3. Mint/Coriander: For topping
  4. Fried Onions: For topping

Step-by-Step: Recipe

  1. Marination: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot (Lagan), mix chicken, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, biryani masala, green chilies, mint, coriander, fried onions, lemon juice, oil, and salt. Let it rest for 1-2 hours (or overnight).
  2. Fry Onions: Slice 3 medium onions thinly and deep fry until golden brown. Set aside.
  3. Prepare Rice: Boil 2-3 liters of water in a large pot. Add whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, shahi jeera) and salt. Add the soaked, drained rice and cook until 70-80% cooked (it should still be firm).
  4. Layering: Spread the marinated chicken evenly at the bottom of the pot. Layer the partially cooked rice directly over the chicken.
  5. Final Topping & Dum: Drizzle saffron-milk, ghee, remaining fried onions, mint, and coriander on top.
  6. Dum Cooking: Seal the pot with aluminum foil or a wheat-flour dough, then put the lid on tightly.
  7. Cook: Place the pot on high heat for 5 minutes, then reduce to very low heat and cook for 25-30 minutes.
  8. Rest & Serve: Remove from heat and let it sit covered for 10-15 minutes before serving to ensure the rice sets.
