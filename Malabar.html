Malabar Biryani

Ingredients

For the Chicken Masala:

  1. Chicken: 500g, cleaned and cut
  2. Onions: 3 medium, thinly sliced
  3. Tomatoes: 2 medium, sliced
  4. Ginger-Garlic-Green Chili Paste: 2 tbsp (crushed)
  5. Curd: Half cup
  6. Spices:Half tbsp Turmeric, Half tbsp Pepper powder, 1 tbsp Garam masala
  7. Herbs: A handful of coriander, mint, and curry leaves
  8. Other: 1 tbsp Lemon juice, 1 tsp Vinegar, Salt, Oil/Ghee for cooking

For the Rice (Kaima):

  1. Kaima/Jeerakasala Rice: 2 cups, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  2. Water: 1:2 ratio (4 cups)
  3. Whole Spices: 2-3 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick, 2 cardamoms
  4. Ghee: 2-3 tbsp

For Garnish & Dum:

  1. Fried Onions: Half cup (sliced thin)
  2. Cashews & Raisins: Fried in ghee

Step-by-Step Recipe

  1. Prepare Chicken Masala: Heat oil/ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot, add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic-green chili paste and sauté until raw smell leaves. Add tomatoes and cook until mushy. Add chicken pieces, salt, turmeric, pepper, and garam masala. Mix in curd, coriander, mint, and curry leaves. Cover and cook for 15-20 minutes until the chicken is tender and gravy is thick.
  2. Prepare Ghee Rice: Heat ghee in a separate pot. Add whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom). Add the drained rice and sauté for 1-2 minutes until it turns aromatic. Add 4 cups of boiling water and salt. Cover and cook on low heat until the water is completely absorbed and the rice is 90% cooked.
  3. Layering & Dum: In the heavy pot with chicken masala, layer the cooked ghee rice on top. Garnish with fried onions, cashews, raisins, and a little ghee. Cover with a tight lid. Use a tawa (griddle) under the pot and cook on very low heat (dum) for 10-15 minutes. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
