Pani Puri

Ingredients

  1. For the Pani (Spiced Water): 1 cup coriander leaves, ½ cup mint leaves, 2-3 green chilies, 1-inch ginger, 1 tbsp roasted cumin powder, 1 tbsp chaat masala, 1 tbsp tamarind pulp, 1 tbsp jaggery, 1 lemon juice, 4 cups cold water, salt, 2 tbsp boondi.
  2. For the Filling: 3 medium potatoes (boiled/mashed), ½ cup yellow peas or chickpeas (boiled), 1 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp chaat masala, salt, coriander leaves.
  3. For the Puri: 1 cup semolina (suji/rava), 1 tbsp maida or wheat flour, oil for frying, pinch of baking soda, water.

Step-by-Step Recipe:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Mix semolina, flour, salt, and baking soda. Knead into a stiff dough using water. Cover with a damp cloth and rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll and Fry Puris: Roll the dough very thin. Cut into small, even circles. Fry in medium-hot oil until golden and crisp, pressing lightly so they puff up. Store in an airtight container once cooled.
  3. Prepare the Pani: Blend coriander, mint, green chilies, ginger, tamarind, jaggery, cumin powder, and chaat masala into a smooth paste. Mix this paste with 4 cups of water, add lemon juice, salt, and boondi.
  4. Prepare the Filling: Mix boiled mashed potatoes, chickpeas, chili powder, chaat masala, and salt.
  5. Assemble: Gently break the top of a puri, stuff it with the potato mixture, dip into the spiced water (pani), and serve immediately.
