Bhatura

Ingredients

  1. All-purpose flour (Maida): 2 cups
  2. Semolina (Sooji/Rava): 1-2 tablespoons (adds slight crispness)
  3. Curd (Yogurt): 1/4 - 1/2 cup (plain, sour is fine)
  4. Baking Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  5. Baking Soda: 1/4 teaspoon
  6. Sugar: 1 teaspoon
  7. Salt: To taste (approx. 1/2 teaspoon)
  8. Oil/Ghee: 1-2 tablespoons (for dough) + for deep frying
  9. Water: As needed for kneading (lukewarm is best)

Step-by-Step Recipe:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, sift together 2 cups of maida, baking powder, and baking soda. Add 1 tablespoon of sooji, salt, and sugar. Mix well.
  2. Knead the Dough: Add 1/4 cup curd and 1-2 tablespoons oil/ghee. Combine to make it crumbly. Gradually add small amounts of water and knead to form a soft, smooth, and slightly sticky dough.
  3. Rest the Dough: Grease the dough with a little oil, cover with a damp cloth or plate, and let it rest in a warm place for 2–4 hours (essential for fermentation).
  4. Shape the Bhaturas: After resting, knead the dough again for 1–2 minutes, then divide into 8–10 equal, smooth balls.
  5. Roll the Bhaturas: Take a dough ball, apply a little oil to it, and roll it out into an oval or round shape using a rolling pin. Keep it medium-thick (not too thin like a poori).
  6. Deep Fry: Heat oil in a kadai over medium-high heat. Once hot (not smoking), gently slide the rolled dough into the oil.
  7. Puff it Up: Press gently with a perforated spoon/spatula until it puffs up like a balloon. Flip it and fry until both sides are golden brown.
  8. Serve: Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with chole (chickpea curry).
