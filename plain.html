Plain Dosa

Ingredients

  1. Rice: 1 cup short-grain or idli rice
  2. Urad Dal: 1/2 cup whole or split skinned urad dalMethi (Fenugreek)
  3. Seeds: 1/4 teaspoon (for flavor and better fermentation)
  4. Poha (Flattened Rice): 1-2 tablespoons (optional, for softness)
  5. Salt: To taste (approx. 1/2 teaspoon)Water: As neededOil or Ghee: For cooking

Step-by-Step Recipe

  1. Soak: Wash the rice and dal 3-4 times. Soak them together with the methi seeds and poha in sufficient water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind:Drain the water and blend the mixture in a blender, adding fresh water little by little until you get a smooth, slightly thick batter.
  3. Ferment: Transfer the batter to a large container. Mix well, cover, and set aside in a warm place for 8-12 hours (or overnight) until it bubbles up and smells slightly sour.
  4. Prepare: Once fermented, gently mix the batter and add salt. If it's too thick, add a little water to reach a pourable, spreadable consistency.
  5. Cook: Heat a non-stick or iron tawa on medium heat. Spread a ladle of batter in a circular motion, starting from the center. Drizzle 1-2 tsp of oil/ghee around the edges.
  6. Serve: Cook until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. If desired, flip and cook for a few seconds, or serve immediately with chutney and sambar.
