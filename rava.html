Rava Dosa

Ingredients

  1. Rava (Semolina/Suji): 1 cup (fine is best)
  2. Rice Flour: 1 cup
  3. All-Purpose Flour (Maida): ½ cup
  4. Curd (Yogurt): ½ cup (optional, adds color)
  5. Water: 4-5 cups (watery consistency)
  6. Aromatics: 1 finely chopped onion, 1-2 green chillies, 1-inch grated ginger, curry leaves, coriander leaves
  7. Spices: 1 tsp cumin seeds, ½ tsp peppercorns (crushed)
  8. Salt: To taste
  9. Oil/Ghee: For roasting

Step-by-Step Recipe

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a large bowl, whisk together rava, rice flour, maida, salt, and curd (if using) with water to ensure no lumps form.
  2. Add Seasoning:Add the chopped onion, green chillies, ginger, coriander leaves, cumin seeds, and crushed pepper.
  3. Rest the Batter: Let the mixture rest for at least 15-30 minutes. Rava absorbs water, so check the consistency after resting; it should be very thin/watery. Add more water if it becomes thick.
  4. Heat the Tawa: Heat a non-stick or iron tawa on high. The pan must be smoking hot. Sprinkle water on it to test; it should sizzle.
  5. Pour the Batter: Stir the batter well before every dosa, as rava settles. Pour the batter starting from the outside edge and moving inwards. Do not spread it with a ladle like a normal dosa; fill the gaps by drizzling more batter.
  6. Cook: Drizzle oil/ghee around the edges and in the center. Cook on medium-high heat until the bottom is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Serve: Gently loosen the edges, fold the dosa, and remove. Do not flip unless you prefer it to be roasted on both sides.
