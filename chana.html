Chana Masala

Ingredients

    For the Chickpeas (Chana):
  1. 1 cup dried Chickpeas (Kabuli Chana), soaked overnight (8-10 hours)
  2. 3-4 cups Water (for boiling)
  3. 1 Bay leaf & 1-inch Cinnamon stick
  4. Salt to taste For the Masala Gravy:
  5. 3 tbsp Oil or Ghee
  6. 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  7. 2 Medium Onions, finely chopped or puréed
  8. 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste
  9. 3 Medium Tomatoes, pureed
  10. 2-3 Green Chillies, sliced
  11. 1 tsp Kashmiri Red Chili Powder
  12. 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  13. 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  14. 1.5 tsp Chana Masala Powder
  15. 1 tsp Amchur Powder (Dried Mango Powder) OR 2 tsp Anardana Powder (Pomegranate Seeds)
  16. 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  17. 1 tsp Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves), crushed
  18. Fresh Coriander leaves, for garnish

Step-by-Step Recipe:

  1. Prepare Chickpeas:Drain the soaked chickpeas and rinse well. Pressure cook with 3 cups of water, salt, bay leaf, and cinnamon for 4-5 whistles until very tender.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil/ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds. Once they crackle, add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Cook Masala Base: Add ginger-garlic paste and green chillies; sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  4. Add Spices: Lower the heat and add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and chana masala powder. Sauté for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Simmer Curry: Add the cooked chickpeas along with the water they were cooked in (use more water if a thinner gravy is desired). Mash a few chickpeas with the back of the spoon to thicken the gravy. Cover and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Final Touches: Stir in the amchur/anardana powder, garam masala, and kasuri methi. Cook for another minute.
  7. Garnish: Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander and lemon juice. Serve hot.
