Veg Biryani

Ingredients

For Rice:

  1. 1.5 cups Aged Basmati Rice (soaked for 30 mins)
  2. Whole spices: 1 bay leaf, 4 green cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon, 1 black cardamom, 2-3 cloves
  3. Salt (as needed)

For Vegetable Masala:

  1. 3-4 tbsp Ghee or Oil
  2. 1 large Onion (thinly sliced)
  3. 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  4. 2-3 Green Chilies (slit)
  5. 2.5 cups Mixed Vegetables (chopped carrots, peas, beans, potatoes)
  6. 1 cup thick Yogurt (Curd), whisked
  7. Spices: 1 tsp Kashmiri Red Chili Powder, ¼ tsp Turmeric, 1.5 tsp Biryani Masala powder, 1 tsp Cumin powder
  8. ¼ cup Mint leaves & ¼ cup Coriander leaves (chopped)

For Garnishing/Layering:

  1. Saffron strands soaked in 3 tbsp hot milk
  2. 2 tbsp Fried onions (Birista)
  3. 1 tsp Rose water (optional)

Step-by-Step Recipe

  1. Prepare the Rice: Rinse 1.5 cups basmati rice until water runs clear, then soak for 30 minutes.Bring 5-6 cups water to a boil with whole spices and salt.Add the soaked, drained rice and cook until it is 75-80% done (should have a slight bite).Drain completely in a colander and set aside.
  2. Sauté Vegetables: Heat ghee/oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add whole spices and fry onions until golden brown.Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; sauté until raw smell disappears.Add vegetables (carrots, potatoes, beans, peas) and sauté for 3-4 minutes on high heat.Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and biryani masala. Mix well.Lower the heat, add the whisked yogurt and salt. Stir continuously until the oil starts leaving the sides.Add chopped mint and coriander leaves.
  3. Layer and Dum Cook: If doing one layer: Smooth the vegetables, top with all the parboiled rice.If doing two layers: Put half the rice, then half the veggies, then the remaining rice.Top with saffron milk, fried onions, extra coriander/mint leaves, and a drizzle of ghee.Seal the pot tightly with aluminum foil or a cloth and a lid to trap steam.Cook on the lowest heat for 15-20 minutes (or place the pot on a hot iron tawa/skillet to avoid burning)
  4. Serving: Let the biryani rest for 10-15 minutes before opening.Gently fluff with a fork from the edge to keep rice grains intact, ensuring a mix of white and masala rice.Serve hot with cucumber raita.
