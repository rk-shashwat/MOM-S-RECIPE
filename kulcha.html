MOM'S RECIPES

Kulcha

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  1. Flour: 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida) or wheat flour
  2. Leavening: 1 tsp baking powder and 1/4 tsp baking soda
  3. Binder/Softener: cup yogurt (curd) and 2 tbsp oil/ghee
  4. Flavorings: 1 tsp sugar, 1/2 tsp salt, and water as needed
  5. Garnish: Nigella seeds (kalonji) or sesame seeds and chopped coriander leaves

Step-by-Step Recipe:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, sugar, baking powder, and baking soda. Add curd and oil, mixing well. Gradually add lukewarm water and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  2. Rest the Dough: Apply a little oil on top, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rest for 1–2 hours to ferment.
  3. Shape the Kulcha: Divide the dough into equal portions. Roll each into a thick round or oval shape (around 4-5 inches).
  4. Add Toppings: Sprinkle coriander leaves and nigella seeds, rolling gently once more to seal them in.
  5. Cook on Tawa: Heat a iron tawa until hot. Apply water to one side of the kulcha, then place that wet side down on the hot tawa.
  6. Add Steam/Cook: Once bubbles appear, cover the pan and cook for 1–2 minutes on low flame, or flip the tawa directly over the flame for a charred, tandoor effect.
  7. Serve: Remove, apply butter or ghee, and serve hot with chole
