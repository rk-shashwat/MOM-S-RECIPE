Dal Makhani

Ingredients

  1. Lentils: 1 cup Whole Black Urad Dal, ¼ cup Rajma (Kidney Beans)
  2. Cooking Liquid: Water or broth (plus 4-5 cups for boiling)
  3. Fat: 3-4 tbsp Butter (or ghee), 1 tbsp oil
  4. Aromatics: 1 large Onion (finely chopped), 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste, 2-3 Green Chilies (slit)
  5. Tomatoes: 2-3 large Tomatoes (pureed) or 1 cup Tomato puree
  6. Spices: 1 tsp Cumin seeds, 1 tsp Kashmiri Red Chili powder, ½ tsp Turmeric powder, 1 tsp Coriander powder, ½ tsp Garam Masala, 1 tsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  7. Finish: 3-4 tbsp Fresh Cream (or Malai)
  8. Garnish: Chopped coriander leaves

Step-by-Step Recipe

  1. Soak & Boil:Rinse the dal and rajma until water runs clear. Soak in plenty of water for at least 8 hours or overnight. Pressure cook with 4 cups of water and a pinch of salt for 6–8 whistles or until beans are extremely soft.
  2. Mash the Lentils: Once cooked, mash the lentils slightly with the back of a ladle to create a creamy texture.
  3. Prepare the Base: In a large pot or kadai, heat butter and oil. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add Aromatics: Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Cook Tomatoes & Spices: Add the tomato puree, Kashmiri red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Cook this masala on medium heat until the fat begins to separate from the sides.
  6. Simmer: Add the boiled and mashed lentils along with the water they were cooked in. Stir well. Add more water to adjust consistency, then cover and simmer on low heat for at least 30–45 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent sticking.
  7. Finish: Stir in kasuri methi and cream. Cook for another 5 minutes. Top with a dollop of butter, garnish with coriander leaves, and serve hot with naan or rice.
