Paper Dosa

Ingredients

  1. 1.5 cups Rice
  2. ½ cup Urad Dal (skinned black gram)
  3. 1 tbsp Chana Dal
  4. ¼ cup Poha (flattened rice)
  5. ¼ tsp Methi Seeds (fenugreek)
  6. Water (as needed)
  7. Salt (to taste)
  8. Oil/Ghee (as needed for roasting)

Step-by-Step Recipe

  1. Soak: Rinse rice and dal separately. Soak rice in one bowl, and lentils with methi seeds in another. Soak poha separately for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Grind: Blend the soaked rice, lentils, and poha into a smooth, fluffy batter, using water sparingly.
  3. Ferment: Add salt, mix well, and cover. Let it ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours, or until it doubles in volume.
  4. Prepare Tawa: Heat a non-stick or cast-iron pan on medium-high. Spread a little oil and sprinkle water, wiping it off to get the right temperature (sizzle test).
  5. Spread Dosa: Pour a ladle of batter in the center. Immediately, spread it quickly in a circular motion, from center to edges, making it as thin as possible.
  6. Roast: Drizzle ghee/oil around the edges and center. Cook on low-medium flame until the bottom is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Serve: Fold and serve immediately, as paper dosa turns soft if not consumed hot.
